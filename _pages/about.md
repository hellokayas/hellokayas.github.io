About Me
------

I am a PhD candidate in the [Department of Computer Science](https://www.mccormick.northwestern.edu/computer-science/) at Northwestern University.

### Large Language Models, Vision-Language Models, and Multimodal AI

I have worked on large language models, vision-language systems, and multimodal AI across scientific and software-engineering applications. This includes studying reproducibility and determinism in hybrid LLM–graph neural network pipelines for materials-property prediction ([Machine Learning: Science and Technology](https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.1088/2632-2153/ae696b/pdf)), evaluating LLMs for inverse semiconductor design through candidate generation and computational validation ([Digital Discovery 2026](https://pubs.rsc.org/en/content/articlelanding/2026/dd/d5dd00544b)), developing customizable LLM-based code-documentation systems using retrieval and parameter-efficient fine-tuning ([JOSS 2025](https://www.theoj.org/joss-papers/joss.07489/10.21105.joss.07489.pdf)), and designing multimodal multiple-choice reasoning systems that use process-of-elimination strategies for visual question answering ([JOSS 2025](https://joss.theoj.org/papers/10.21105/joss.07783)).

### Recommendation and Ranking Systems

I have worked on recommendation and ranking systems that incorporate long-term behavioral sequences, multimodal product information, time constraints, and historical evidence. My work includes a resource-efficient framework for long-sequence recommendation using sliding-window training ([Rev-AI 2026](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2604.12372)), multimodal sequential recommendation that combines visual and textual product context for next-item prediction ([Computing Conference 2026](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.06458)), reinforcement-learning strategies for e-commerce recommendation under limited user time budgets and item-evaluation costs ([ICPRAM 2026](https://www.scitepress.org/Papers/2026/142980/142980.pdf)), and temporal screening and ranking methods that use evidence from past observations to prioritize future candidates ([DATA 2026](https://data.scitevents.org/)).

### Reinforcement Learning and Sequential Decision-Making

I have worked on reinforcement learning and sequential decision-making in nonstationary and high-frequency environments. This includes group-aware policy optimization for high-frequency directional trading using order-book information ([DATA 2026](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.25527)) and adaptive model selection for offline contextual Markov decision processes without assuming stationarity ([TMLR](https://openreview.net/pdf?id=FGBZ4q1HPZ)).

### Machine Learning for Materials Science

I have worked on machine-learning methods for accelerating materials characterization, simulation, and property prediction. This includes surrogate models for predicting material stress–strain curves faster than crystal-plasticity finite-element simulations ([ICMLA 2025](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/11471533)), deep-learning models for predicting lattice parameters of two-dimensional materials from atomic-resolution images ([The Journal of Physical Chemistry C](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.jpcc.5c04792)), and time-series models for learning processing–microstructure–property relationships in polycrystalline materials ([Scientific Reports 2025](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-025-06894-x)).

### Federated and Personalized Learning

I have worked on personalized federated learning methods that cluster clients using embeddings derived from their model-loss patterns, enabling models to adapt to heterogeneous client populations ([ICML 2026](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2506.22427)).

### Statistical Learning Theory and Algorithms

I have worked on theoretical and algorithmic problems in statistics and machine learning. This includes convergence guarantees for resampled studentized quantile estimators and the m-out-of-n bootstrap ([NeurIPS 2025](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2505.11725)), a simple single-pass and memory-efficient approximation algorithm for correlation clustering ([NeurIPS 2023](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2305.13560.pdf)), and consistency guarantees for maximum-likelihood estimation in probabilistic principal component analysis under rotational non-identifiability ([NeurIPS 2023](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2311.05046.pdf)).

### Applications in Law and Ethics

I have worked on probabilistic judicial decision-support systems that reason over uncertain and potentially conflicting evidence. This includes a patented computing system for aggregating witness statements and identifying likely logically consistent case scenarios ([U.S. Patent Application 20250111457, 2025](https://www.freepatentsonline.com/y2025/0111457.html)) and algorithms for finding the *k* most likely judicial worlds under logical constraints and uncertain witness credibility ([SUM 2024](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-031-76235-2_5)). My work related to Fairness in AI can be found [here](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.12337).

### Adversarial Machine Learning and Review-Fraud Detection

I have worked on adversarial machine learning for review-fraud detection, focusing on attacks and defenses that evolve over time. This includes dynamically adaptive defenses against changing attacks on review-fraud detection engines ([IEEE Transactions on Computational Social Systems 2023](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/10285721)) and dynamically changing attacks together with adaptive ensemble defenses, which received a Best Paper Award ([DASC/PiCom/CBDCom/CyberSciTech 2022](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/abstract/document/9927814)).

### Mathematics and Mathematical Problem Solving

I have also worked on mathematical problem solving and number theory, including the analysis of logical number-guessing games and optimal questioning strategies ([Pi in the Sky 2021](https://media.pims.math.ca/pi_in_sky/pi22.pdf)) and the iterated divisor function and its possible relationship with highly composite numbers ([International Workshop for Young Mathematicians: Number Theory 2017](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/316596873_The_Repeated_Divisor_Function_and_Possible_Correlation_with_Highly_Composite_Numbers)).
